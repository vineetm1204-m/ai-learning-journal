## 🧠 Concept Explanation

BERT (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers) is a pretraining method that learns deep bidirectional representations by masking random tokens in a sentence and predicting them. Unlike traditional left‑to‑right language models, BERT sees the full context on both sides of each token because the Transformer encoder attends to all positions simultaneously. During pretraining, 15 % of tokens are replaced: 80 % with a special [MASK] token, 10 % with a random token, and 10 % left unchanged. The model is trained with a masked language modeling (MLM) loss (cross‑entropy over the vocabulary) and a next‑sentence prediction (NSP) loss that teaches it to understand sentence relationships. After pretraining on large corpora (e.g., Wikipedia + BookCorpus), the encoder can be fine‑tuned on downstream tasks such as classification, QA, or NER by adding a task‑specific head. The bidirectional nature gives BERT a strong advantage on tasks requiring full‑sentence understanding.

---

## ✅ Key Points

- Uses a Transformer encoder stack with full self‑attention for bidirectional context.
- Masked Language Modeling (MLM) predicts randomly masked tokens (15 % masking rate).
- Next Sentence Prediction (NSP) teaches inter‑sentence coherence.
- Pre‑trained on massive unlabeled text, then fine‑tuned with task‑specific heads.
- Achieves state‑of‑the‑art on many NLP benchmarks with relatively little task data.

## 💡 Real-World Analogy

BERT is like a student who reads an entire paragraph, blanks out a few words, and then fills them in using the surrounding context on both sides.

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

- Treating BERT as a unidirectional model and feeding only left‑to‑right context.
- Forgetting to add the [CLS] token for classification tasks or using the wrong pooling strategy.
- Using the pretrained tokenizer inconsistently (e.g., different vocab or lower‑casing) between pretraining and fine‑tuning.
